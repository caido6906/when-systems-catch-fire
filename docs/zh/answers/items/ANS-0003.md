## 问题 / Question

中文：黑洞信息到底是全丢了，还是能以某种方式保住？
English: Does black-hole information disappear entirely, or can it be preserved in some form?

## 新答案 / New Answer

中文：更合理的答案是部分丢失、部分保留；视界更像压缩与门控，而不是简单抹除。
English: The better answer is partial loss and partial retention; the horizon behaves more like compression and gating than simple erasure.

## 已有答案或旧问题背景 / Existing Answers or Prior Problem Background

中文：传统争论在“完全丢失”与“完全守恒”之间对撞，常常没有中间态。
English: The classic debate pits complete loss against complete conservation and often leaves no middle state.

## 新解释在哪里 / What Is New in This Answer

中文：这里把视界当作门控面：信息在穿越前后经历压缩、编码和可恢复性的变化，因此不必是二元结论。
English: The novelty is to treat the horizon as a gating surface: information changes compression, encoding, and recoverability across it, so the outcome need not be binary.

## 推出依据 / Basis

中文：来源笔记明确给出“黑洞信息丢失是乘法门控的数学必然，但不是完全丢失”。
English: The source note explicitly says black-hole information loss is a mathematical necessity of multiplicative gating but not total loss.

## 可检验性 / Testability

中文：如果视界物理、蒸发谱或类比黑洞实验能区分“完全丢失”和“部分保留”，这个答案就可被检验。
English: If horizon physics, evaporation spectra, or analogue-black-hole experiments can distinguish total loss from partial retention, this answer becomes testable.
